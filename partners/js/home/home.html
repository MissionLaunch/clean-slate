Sealing and Expungement for Adults

Sealing of Misdemeanor Convictions and Non-convictions under D.C. 16-803
Add your client's entire criminal record for analysis under D.C. Code § 16-803. D.C. Code § 16-803 authorizes sealing of records not resulting in conviction, as well as records of conviction for certain non-serious misdemeanors, and of felony failure to appear. Misdemeanors that are defined as “ineligible” for sealing are listed in § 16-801(9), and include DUI and driver’s license offenses, intra-family offenses, sex offenses, a variety of misdemeanor fraud offenses (including credit card, public assistance, and insurance fraud), and building code violations

Sealing An Arrest Record On The Grounds Of Actual Innocence under D.C. Code § 16-802
Any type of offense – felony or misdemeanor – is eligible for relief under this type of motion. And this type of motion can be filed immediately after termination of the prosecution.
Seal record of a Non-conviction arrests as a fugitive for justice under § 16-803.01.
A person arrested upon a warrant issued pursuant to § 23-701 or arrested within the District of Columbia as a fugitive from justice without a warrant having been issued may file a motion to seal the record of the District of Columbia arrest and related Superior Court proceedings at any time after the person has appeared before the proper official in the jurisdiction from which he or she was a fugitive.
Seal decriminalized/legalized offenses (like Marijuana) under § 16-803.02
District of Columbia Official Code to allow individuals to file a motion to seal the records of offenses that are decriminalized or legalized after the date of the arrest, charge, or conviction; to place the burden of proof on the prosecutor to show that the movant’s record is not eligible for sealing because the conduct was not decriminalized or legalized.

Sealing and Expungement for Juveniles

Sealing of juvenile delinquency proceedings under D.C. Code § 16-2335
A person who has been the subject of juvenile proceedings in D.C. can also file a motion to have all records associated with the case sealed. The person needs to wait until two years after the person has been released from court supervision. In addition, the person cannot have been subsequently convicted of a crime or adjudicated delinquent/in need of supervision. Upon entry of the order, the proceedings will be treated as if they never occurred.
Expungement record of probation under D.C. Code 48-904.01(e)
If the client is charged with any type of possession of drug in DC and they were not been convicted in any other jurisdiction in the country then they are eligible for probation under 48-904.01(e). Applicable only if convicted of a possession offence. It cannot be possession with intent to distribute any drug or distribution. And. The probation must be under one year. They must complete any conditions set by the court.
