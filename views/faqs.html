What it means to seal your criminal history

How long would it take to seal my criminal history?

Sealing can take between 4 months and a year. This short video explains the process.

What does it mean to seal a DC Criminal Record? How can I learn more about sealing.

If a DC criminal record is sealed, the government deletes or limits access to it. A sealed record can generally not be used against you on job applications, in other background checks, or in certain legal proceedings. Even after your DC criminal record is sealed, there are certain agencies and employers that CAN still see your record.

Starting the process of sealing your criminal history

Who can help me seal my criminal history record? How can they help?

A lawyer can look at your entire criminal history record and determine what specific arrests or convictions can be sealed. If it turns out that you are eligible to seal part of your criminal history record, a lawyer can also help you draft a motion, which is a letter to the court requesting them to seal part of your criminal history. Even if they can’t write the entire motion for you, they can review it before you submit it to the court.

Other options outside of sealing your record

Is there anything that can prevent an employer from asking about my criminal record on an application?

Employers are able to ask. BUT, a law was passed in 2014 whereby an employer should not be asking about your criminal history record. You can also report that employer; watch the video to find out how. This law does not apply to all employers, however.
